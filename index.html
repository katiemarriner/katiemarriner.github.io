    Mental Health in North Carolina
    HighCharts, DataTables, Tabletop.js, GoogleMaps

    This data visualization was created using Highcharts, Tabletop.js with GoogleMaps and DataTables with data from datahub.io. It was important that I let the data tell the story. North Carolina has been in the news recently for its lack of mental health facilities, as is the rest of the United States. The state is divided into three regions, the Piedmont holds the Research Triange Park and the largest city of Charlotte. Outside of the piedmont region, to the east is the eastern region and to the west, the mountain region. Both are predominantly rural. The American Psychological Association (APA) released a statement about the need for psychologists in rural regions of the United States. The data I presented shows just that. I divided the data between facilities in the three regions. In order to get a better picture of the care available by region, I used the drilldown Highchart to divide the regions into the types of facilities in each.

    Comedy in NYC
    APIs: Twitter, GoogleMaps, Instagram, Yelp

    This API Mashup was created using the Twitter, Instagram, Yelp and Google Maps APIs. I love stand-up comedy, but comedy clubs are often hole-in-the-wall places that are difficult to find. Using an article by NYC Go, I identified the top comedy clubs in New York City and geotagged each one. I made the geotagged locations correspond to their Twitter accounts and Instagram tags to gather information and capture the atmosphere of the club. The Yelp API is used to get a longer list of clubs to visit.

    UNC Baseball
    Python, Django

    The UNC Baseball Roster was built using Python with the Django framework. I focused on usability and the ease at which the roster could be read. Often times, rosters for sports teams are clunky and hard to read. My goal was to recreate a roster that is clean and readable. There were many challenges I had to overcome when creating the application. I had to decide what information to add when there was none available. The freshmen on the baseball team do not have a summary of previous years or images on file. I had to design the application in a way that would indicate information would be forthcoming as soon as their first season ended. The absense of images left pages feeling blank. That is one of the many reasons I included Twitter feeds to each of the player's pages.
