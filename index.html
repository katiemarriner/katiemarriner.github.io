The SEC is cracking down on made-up earnings numbers. We crunched the numbers — it hasn't helped

Python/pandas

After our initial graphic on unaudited earnings metrics, MarketWatch partnered with Audit Analytics to get data on all S&P 500 companies. We found that after the SEC's crackdown in May of 2016, companies have failed to change how they report earnings. Because the data must be entered by hand as there is no defined category by the SEC to identify a company's non-GAAP metrics, I looked at the data extensively by cross-checking numbers from different sources to verify.

Beat the Fed

Python

I parsed the data for a simulation that lets users move the inflation rate of different sub-categories of PCE inflation. Moving these sub-categories shows how much impact each has on the overall inflation rate and what it would take for the Fed to reach its target rate of 2%.

Search Hillary Clinton's emails

I implemented Bootstrap Tables and server-side pagination to improve load time. Features include the ability to search by certain senders/recipients and popular search terms to guide users on what to search for.
